# Review Conclusion Guide

## Conclusion Types Explained

### 修后再审
- **Meaning**: Revise and resubmit for re-review
- **Implication**: Major revisions required
- **Next Step**: Manuscript returns to same reviewers after revision
- **Typical Scenarios**:
  - Methodology concerns requiring clarification
  - Missing data or analysis
  - Significant structural issues
  - Insufficient literature review

### 可刊用
- **Meaning**: Acceptable for publication
- **Implication**: Minor revisions needed
- **Next Step**: Proceed to editorial final review after revisions
- **Typical Scenarios**:
  - Minor language corrections
  - Small formatting adjustments
  - Additional citations suggested
  - Clarification requests

### 拒稿
- **Meaning**: Reject
- **Implication**: Not suitable for publication
- **Next Step**: Manuscript rejected, author may submit elsewhere
- **Typical Scenarios**:
  - Scope mismatch with journal
  - Fundamental methodological flaws
  - Insufficient originality
  - Ethical concerns

### 同意录用
- **Meaning**: Agree to publish
- **Implication**: Manuscript accepted
- **Next Step**: Production and publication process begins

## Similarity Ratio Standards

- **Target**: Typically under 10%
- **Measurement**: Text similarity/copy ratio
- **Tools**: Various plagiarism detection software
- **Handling Exceedances**:
  - Identify specific problematic sections
  - Require paraphrasing or proper citation
  - Re-check after revision

## Abstract Requirements

### Chinese Abstract
- **Standard length**: 500-800 characters
- **Must include**: Research purpose, methods, key findings, conclusions

### English Abstract
- **Must correspond** to Chinese abstract content
- **Length**: Typically 150-300 words
- **Structure**: Background, methods, results, conclusions
